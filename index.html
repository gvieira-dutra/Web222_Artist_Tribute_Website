Snoop Dogg's life and career

Career

Snoop Dogg build his career as a multidimensional professional. He's worn many hats throughout his career, on top of being one of the most demanded DJ's in the world, he is also a rapper, singer, songwriter, producer, shows host, entrepreneur and actor. He was born on October 20, 1971, at 6:20PM, in Long Beach, California and his birth name is Calvin Cordizar Broadus Jr. Snoop Dogg began his career in the early 90s when he was discovered by Dr. Dre, a notable record producer and rapper.


His first album, entitled "Doggystyle", was released in 1993 in partnership with Death Row Records. It was an instant success, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200 chart and selling close to 1 million copies in its first week. The album helped establish Snoop Dogg as a major force in hip hop and included hits that are known today as his most classic songs such as "Gin and Juice" and "What's My Name". Snoop Dogg released his second album in 1996, "Tha Doggfather". However, the album was not as successful as his first album, "Doggystyle" Snoop Dogg then decided to leave Death Row Records and signed with No Limit Records in 1998. His third album was released this same year, titled "Da Game Is to Be Sold, Not to Be Told" with the contribution of several artists such as Dr. Dre, Warren G, Nate Dogg among others.

Over the next few years, Snoop Dogg released several albums, including "No Limit Top Dogg", "Tha Last Meal", and "Paid tha Cost to Be da Boss". He also began his acting career around this time, appearing in films like "I Got The Hook Up", 1998 "Half Baked" 1998, "Ride" 1998, and "Urban Manace" 1999. Snoop Dogg also hosted his television show, "Doggy Fizzle Televizzle," which aired on MTV in 2002.

Beyond Music

In addition to having acted in several movies, he has also worked as an executive producer in the following movies. "Tha Eastsidaz" 2000, "The Wash" 2001, "Hood of Horror" 2006, and "When Claude Got Shot" 2021, among others.

In 2004, Snoop Dogg released his seventh album, "R&G (Rhythm & Gangsta): The Masterpiece". The album included hit singles like "Drop It Like It's Hot" and "Signs" and received critical acclaim. The album also marked a return to the top for Snoop Dogg, as his previous albums were not as successful as a singer would usually like them to be.

Snoop Dogg continued to release successful albums throughout the late 2000s and 2010s, including "Tha Blue Carpet Treatment", "Ego Trippin'", and "Doggumentary". He also collaborated with several artists, including Pharrell Williams, Katy Perry, and Wiz Khalifa. In addition to his music career, Snoop Dogg continued to act, appearing in films like "Soul Plane" 2004, "The Big Bang" 2011 and "Mac & Devin Go to High School" 2012.

Current Endeavors

Despite his controversial past, Snoop Dogg has become one of the most successful rappers of all time. He has sold over 35 million records worldwide and has won numerous awards, including three MTV Video Music Awards and two BET Awards. He has also been inducted into the Hip Hop Hall of Fame and the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
